**Register: Romantic Drama.** The real conflict is always emotional. The shape of the story: a connection → distance → longing → a misunderstanding → a reunion.

The characters are emotionally vulnerable, searching, and incomplete — two people who each hold a piece the other is missing.

Escalate inward: a practical conflict keeps turning into an emotional one; the external problem is only ever a mask for the feeling underneath.

Dialogue is personal, intimate, and heartfelt — close and quiet rather than grand.

Visual language: rain, trains, sunsets, music carrying what words can't.

Climax rule: the emotional truth is finally spoken aloud.
